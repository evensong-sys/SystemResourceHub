## The Reality

Simply Plural did a very specific mix of things:

- front tracking
- member management
- mobile-first design
- easy syncing

Most alternatives only do some of those well, not all of them.

So instead of asking “what replaces Simply Plural,” the better question is:

**what parts of Simply Plural do you actually need?**

---

## If front Tracking is Your Main Need

These are the closest matches:

### Ampersand

- Very similar conceptually to Simply Plural
- Built specifically for systems
- Still evolving, but one of the closest replacements right now

### Hivemind

- Also focused on system tracking
- Comparable feature set
- Worth trying alongside Ampersand to see which feels better

### Ourcana

- Another system-focused app
- Similar category as the above two
- Still developing, so expect some rough edges

---

## If You Care Most about Privacy

### Antar

- Fully local, no accounts
- Very private
- Not built specifically for plurality, so you’ll be adapting it

### Anytype

- Local-first with encrypted sync
- Extremely flexible
- More of a “build your own system” tool than a ready-made solution

These are good if your main concern is control over your data, not convenience.

---

## If You Use Discord Heavily

### PluralKit

- Already widely used
- Strong for member management and proxying
- Not a standalone app, so it doesn’t replace the mobile experience

This works best as part of a setup, not as your only tool.

---

## What You Should Actually Do

Don’t try to pick the “best” app immediately.

Instead:

1. Figure out what you actually used Simply Plural for
    - front logging
    - member profiles
    - switching history
    - social features
2. Try 1–2 options that match that
3. Give yourself time to adjust

You might end up using:

- one app for tracking
- another for profiles
- PluralKit for Discord

That’s normal for now. At least until someone else integrates similar features.

---

## About Simply Plural

Simply Plural is being discontinued, with servers expected to remain up until at least June 1, 2026. After that, online features will stop working and data will be deleted unless exported.

If you’re still using it, don’t wait too long to start testing alternatives.

---

## Final Note

This space is changing fast right now.

New tools are being built, existing ones are adapting, and things will probably look different in a few months.

You don’t have to get it perfect on the first try. You just need something that works for you.
